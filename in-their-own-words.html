In Their Own Words

The following are selected examples of how independent third parties described the work of H. B. F. Antaki — patent attorneys writing under legal obligation to accuracy, review authors summarising the state of a field, encyclopaedia editors, and researchers building on his chemistry. None had any obligation to mention him. These are their words, not ours.

Patents

Patent text is in the public domain. Every word in a patent filing is deliberate, reviewed by attorneys, and legally consequential.

H. B. F. Antaki’s papers (1951, 1958, and especially 1963) have been cited as prior art in 20 verified pharmaceutical patents filed by ten independent companies across six countries between 1970 and 2016. This number continues to grow as more patents are reviewed.

The selected examples below are those in which the patent drafters went beyond a minimal citation and used more explicit or detailed language about his contributions.

Pfizer — US 4,041,163 (1977)
"The pyrimido[2,1-b]benzothiazole ring system was first reported by Antaki, et al., J. Chem. Soc., 551 (1951)."
Declarative. No hedge. "First."

Pfizer — US 4,066,766A (1978)
"The synthesis of a 1H-pyrimido[1,2-a]quinoline appears to have first been reported by Antaki et al., J. Chem. Soc., pp. 551–555 (1951)."
Followed by a detailed paragraph-by-paragraph technical summary of both the 1951 J. Chem. Soc. and the 1958 J. Am. Chem. Soc. papers, concluding: "The compound demonstrated antischistosomal action."

Pfizer — US 4,017,625 (1977)
Cites 1951 J. Chem. Soc. and 1958 J. Am. Chem. Soc.. Uses the word "first" in the Description of Prior Art.

Bristol-Myers — US 4,209,620A (1980)
"Some examples of the acrylates of formula VI are disclosed by Antaki in J. Amer. Chem. Soc., 80, 3066 (1958)."
Listed first.

ICI (Imperial Chemical Industries) — US 5,258,390 / EP 0539153 (1991–1993)
"The compounds ... are known, for example from… Antaki, J. Chem. Soc., 4877 (1963)."
"Suitable reaction conditions are also reported by Antaki in J. Chem. Soc., 4877 (1963)."
Cited twice in the same family.

Zeneca — EP 0539154B1 (1997)
"Suitable reaction conditions are also reported by Antaki in J. Chem. Soc., 4877 (1963)."

E. R. Squibb — US 4,022,897A (1977)
Only one non-patent citation in the entire document: Antaki, J. Chem. Soc., 551 (1951).

Gilead Sciences — US 8,716,319B2 (2014)
Examiner-supplied citation to Antaki’s work on the PTO-892 form.

Review Literature

Bossert & Vater — Medicinal Research Reviews, 1989
DOI: 10.1002/med.2610090304

"hexahydroquinoline derivatives aroused our interest."

Bossert and Vater cited Antaki’s 1963 paper twice in this influential review — first as reference 7, directly after stating that hexahydroquinoline derivatives aroused their interest, and again as reference 13 when placing his work in the historical synthetic lineage alongside the classic Hantzsch and Knoevenagel reactions. This review has been cited thousands of times.

Hermecz & Mészáros — Advances in Heterocyclic Chemistry, Vol. 33, 1983
DOI: 10.1016/S0065-2725(08)60055-0
The canonical review of pyrido[1,2-a]pyrimidine chemistry. Antaki is discussed analytically across multiple pages as structural corrector, priority figure, and first descriptor of several products.

Shur & Israelstam — Journal of Organic Chemistry, 1972
"Antaki and Petrow showed that the product was in fact the 2-methyl-4-keto isomer."
Cited by name in the running text as foundational authority.

Stankevich et al. — Chemistry of Heterocyclic Compounds, 1967
DOI: 10.1007/BF00509440
Cited Antaki 1963 as reference 3.

Reference Works

Antaki 1958 J. Am. Chem. Soc. listed as the primary reference (1a) for Ethyl Ethoxymethyleneacetoacetate.

Allen et al. (Kodak Research Laboratories) — Journal of the American Chemical Society, 1959
Actively prepared and used compounds following the Antaki & Petrow procedure.

Okuda et al. — Yakugaku Zasshi, 1963
DOI: 10.1248/yakushi1947.83.7_679
Survey found only two prior compounds in the class — both synthesised by Antaki (1951 and 1958).
